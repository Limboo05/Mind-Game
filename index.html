What just happened to you

When your brain was told to count blue objects, it entered a state of selective attention — actively suppressing everything that wasn't blue. The yellow vase became cognitively invisible. This is the same mechanism behind the Invisible Gorilla Experiment: your brain doesn't show you reality, it shows you a filtered version based on what it expects to find.
